# Elegant Weddings — Guest Count Policy

*This is fictional sample policy text created for demonstration of EventSense AI.*

## Why Guest Count Matters

The guest count drives many parts of a wedding: catering quantities, seating,
decoration scale, and venue setup. To keep your celebration smooth, we manage guest
count changes through clear deadlines and a simple confirmation process.

## The Guest Count Deadline

**Guest count changes must be confirmed at least 10 days before the event.** This
deadline gives our planning team and your vendors enough time to adjust catering,
seating, and setup without last-minute pressure or errors.

By the 10-day deadline, we ask each couple to confirm a final guest count in writing.
Small natural changes happen, and we account for that during planning, but the
confirmed number at the deadline is the figure we use to finalize catering and
seating arrangements.

## Changes Before the Deadline

Before the 10-day deadline, couples can adjust their guest count freely as plans
develop. We recommend keeping us updated as numbers shift so that catering estimates
and seating plans stay accurate. Increases and decreases before the deadline are
handled as normal planning updates.

## Increases After the Deadline

**Guest count increases after the deadline require manager approval and may affect
catering availability.** Once the 10-day deadline has passed, catering quantities and
seating have usually been finalized with vendors. Adding guests at that point is not
guaranteed.

When a couple requests an increase after the deadline, a planner escalates the
request to a manager. The manager checks whether catering can still accommodate the
additional guests, whether seating and setup can be adjusted, and whether any
additional cost applies. Late increases may not be possible if the catering vendor
cannot supply the extra covers in time.

## Decreases After the Deadline

Reducing the guest count after the deadline is often possible, but it may not reduce
costs that have already been committed with vendors. Catering and other arrangements
based on the confirmed number may already be locked in. We will always try to apply
reductions where the vendor allows it.

## How to Confirm Your Guest Count

1. Review your guest list and finalize attendance as the date approaches.
2. Send your confirmed guest count in writing before the 10-day deadline.
3. We confirm receipt and lock the number for catering and seating.
4. If you need a change after the deadline, contact us so a planner can escalate the
   request for manager review.

## Children, Plus-Ones, and Vendors

When confirming your count, please include children who require a seat or meal,
confirmed plus-ones, and any vendor meals that the venue or caterer counts. A clear,
complete number helps us avoid shortfalls on the day.

## Special and Custom Requests

Some guest-related requests fall outside our standard process — for example, unusual
seating arrangements, special dietary setups at scale, or additional guest services.
**Unsupported or custom requests should be escalated to staff review** so a planner
can confirm feasibility and any cost before it is promised. Note that **the agency
does not provide guest airport transportation unless it is added as a custom
service.**

## Summary of Key Rules

- Confirm guest count changes at least 10 days before the event.
- Before the deadline, changes are handled as normal updates.
- Increases after the deadline require manager approval and may affect catering
  availability.
- Custom guest requests are escalated to staff review.
