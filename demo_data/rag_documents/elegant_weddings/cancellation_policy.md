# Elegant Weddings — Cancellation Policy

*This is fictional sample policy text created for demonstration of EventSense AI.*

## Overview

Plans can change, and we treat cancellations with care and respect. This policy
explains how Elegant Weddings handles cancellation requests for confirmed bookings,
who reviews them, and what couples can expect.

## When This Policy Applies

This policy applies once a booking is confirmed. A booking is confirmed after the
deposit has been received and recorded and a written booking confirmation has been
sent. Before confirmation, no date is held and there is nothing to cancel.

## Cancellation Requests Must Be in Writing

To cancel a confirmed booking, the couple must send a written cancellation request
that includes the booking reference and the wedding date. We use the written request
as the official record and the starting point of our review.

## Manager Review

**Cancellation requests after confirmation must be reviewed by a manager.** A planner
cannot finalize a cancellation on their own. Once a written request is received, it
is escalated to a manager who reviews the booking, the timeline, any vendor
commitments already made, and the deposit terms.

The manager review ensures consistency and fairness, and protects both the couple and
the agency. We aim to acknowledge cancellation requests promptly and to complete the
manager review within a few business days.

## Effect on the Deposit

The booking deposit is non-refundable after booking confirmation. This remains true
when a couple cancels. Because the deposit reserves our planning team and removes the
date from availability, it is not refunded on cancellation. Full deposit terms are
described in the Deposit Policy.

## Effect on the Final Balance

If the final balance has not yet been paid, cancellation ends the obligation to pay
the remaining balance for planning services not yet delivered, subject to manager
review of any costs already committed on the couple's behalf. The final balance is
normally due 14 days before the event; cancelling before that date does not create a
refund of the non-refundable deposit.

If the final balance has already been paid, the manager review determines what, if
any, portion may be returned after accounting for services already delivered and
vendor costs already committed.

## Vendor and Third-Party Costs

Some vendor arrangements made on the couple's behalf may carry their own cancellation
terms. Where third-party costs have already been committed, those costs are the
couple's responsibility and are considered during the manager review.

## Rescheduling Instead of Cancelling

In many cases, rescheduling is a better option than cancelling. If a new date is
available for our planning team, we will do our best to move the booking. The
existing deposit can usually be applied to the new date. Rescheduling requests are
also reviewed by a manager and confirmed in writing.

## Exceptional Circumstances

We understand that serious and unexpected events occur. Couples may describe
exceptional circumstances in their written request. Staff cannot waive the deposit or
approve exceptions on their own. **Unsupported or custom requests should be escalated
to staff review**, and any exception must be approved by a manager and recorded in
writing.

## What Couples Can Expect

1. Send a written cancellation request with the booking reference.
2. We acknowledge the request and escalate it for manager review.
3. A manager reviews the booking, timeline, and committed costs.
4. We confirm the outcome in writing, including any reschedule option.

## Summary of Key Rules

- Cancellations apply only to confirmed bookings.
- Cancellation requests after confirmation must be reviewed by a manager.
- The confirmed deposit is non-refundable.
- Rescheduling is available subject to team availability and manager review.
- Exceptions require manager approval in writing.
