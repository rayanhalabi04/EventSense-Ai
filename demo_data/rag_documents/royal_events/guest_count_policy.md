# Royal Events Agency — Guest Count Policy

*This is fictional sample policy text created for demonstration of EventSense AI.*

## Why Guest Count Matters

For a luxury production, the guest count shapes catering, seating, floor layout,
lighting coverage, and the scale of the bridal entrance. We manage guest count
changes with a clear deadline so that the production and vendors stay accurate.

## The Guest Count Deadline

**Guest count changes are allowed up to 7 days before the event.** Up to the 7-day
mark, clients can adjust their guest count as plans firm up. We ask clients to confirm
a final number in writing by this deadline so catering, seating, and production
coverage can be finalized.

Because our coordination is premium and hands-on, this 7-day window gives our
producers and vendors time to adjust quantities and layout while keeping quality high.

## Changes Before the Deadline

Before the 7-day deadline, increases and decreases are treated as normal production
updates. We recommend sending updates as your list changes so catering estimates,
seating charts, and lighting coverage stay aligned with the real number.

## Increases After the Deadline

**Guest count increases after the 7-day deadline require catering and venue
approval.** Once inside the final 7 days, catering quantities and venue layout are
generally locked. Adding guests at that stage is not guaranteed.

When a client requests a late increase, our producer checks with both the catering
vendor and the venue. The increase can proceed only if catering can supply the extra
covers and the venue can accommodate the additional seating and floor space within
the production design. Additional cost may apply, and timing may make some late
increases impossible.

## Decreases After the Deadline

Reducing the guest count inside the final 7 days is often possible but may not reduce
costs already committed with the caterer or venue. Production elements scaled to the
confirmed number may already be locked in.

## How to Confirm Your Guest Count

1. Finalize attendance, including children needing a seat or meal and confirmed
   plus-ones.
2. Send your confirmed guest count in writing by the 7-day deadline.
3. We lock the number for catering, seating, and production coverage.
4. For a late increase, contact your producer so they can seek catering and venue
   approval.

## VIP Guests and Logistics

For VIP guests, additional services may be requested as paid add-ons. For example,
**the agency can arrange VIP guest airport transportation only as a paid add-on.**
VIP logistics should be confirmed alongside your guest count so the production can
plan arrivals and seating appropriately.

## Concerns and Escalation

If a client is unhappy with how a late guest count request is handled — for instance,
when a significant late increase cannot be accommodated — we want to resolve it
quickly. **High-risk client complaints should be escalated to a manager** for review.

## Summary of Key Rules

- Guest count changes are allowed up to 7 days before the event.
- Before the deadline, changes are normal production updates.
- Increases after the 7-day deadline require catering and venue approval.
- VIP airport transportation is available only as a paid add-on.
- Serious complaints are escalated to a manager.
