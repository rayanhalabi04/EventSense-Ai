# Royal Events Agency — Cancellation Policy

*This is fictional sample policy text created for demonstration of EventSense AI.*

## Overview

Royal Events Agency produces high-end weddings that require advance commitment of
crew, lighting, staging, and vendor resources. This policy explains how cancellations
are handled and how the timing of a cancellation affects the deposit.

## When This Policy Applies

This policy applies once a booking is confirmed and a deposit has been recorded. The
cancellation date is the date we receive the client's written cancellation request.

## How to Cancel

To cancel, the client must send a written cancellation request that includes the
booking reference and the event date. We use the date this request is received to
determine which refund terms apply.

## Refund Terms Based on Timing

Our deposit refund depends on how far in advance the cancellation occurs:

- **The deposit is partially refundable if cancellation happens more than 30 days
  before the event.** A partial refund is issued after deducting production planning
  work and any committed costs.

- **The deposit is non-refundable if cancellation happens within 30 days of the
  event.** In the final 30 days, crew, equipment, lighting, and vendor commitments
  are typically locked, so the deposit cannot be returned.

Full deposit details are in the Deposit Policy.

## Effect on the Final Balance

The final package balance is due 10 days before the event. If a client cancels before
the balance is paid, the timing-based deposit rules above still apply. If the balance
has already been paid, any return beyond the deposit rules is assessed based on
production work completed and vendor costs already committed.

## Vendor and Production Commitments

Lighting rigs, staging, entrance setups, and catering are often reserved well ahead
of the event. Where these costs are committed, they are the client's responsibility
and are factored into any refund calculation.

## Rescheduling

Where our production calendar allows, rescheduling to a new date is often preferable
to cancelling. The existing deposit can usually be applied to the new date.
Rescheduling is subject to production team and equipment availability and is confirmed
in writing.

## Complaints and Escalation

Cancellations can be emotional, and disagreements about refunds may arise. We aim to
handle every case fairly and calmly. **High-risk client complaints should be escalated
to a manager** — for example, when a client is highly dissatisfied, threatens to
dispute a payment, or raises a serious concern about the service. A manager reviews
the situation, confirms the applicable terms, and works toward a resolution.

Staff cannot waive the timing-based deposit rules on their own. Any exception requires
manager approval and is recorded in writing.

## What Clients Can Expect

1. Send a written cancellation request with the booking reference and event date.
2. We record the date the request is received and apply the correct refund tier.
3. We calculate any partial refund after committed costs.
4. If the matter is sensitive or disputed, it is escalated to a manager.
5. We confirm the outcome, including any reschedule option, in writing.

## Summary of Key Rules

- More than 30 days before the event: deposit is partially refundable.
- Within 30 days of the event: deposit is non-refundable.
- Final balance is due 10 days before the event.
- High-risk client complaints are escalated to a manager.
- Exceptions require manager approval in writing.
