EventSense Design System

1. Brand Overview
   Brand Positioning
   EventSense is a premium operations platform for wedding planners, event agencies, venues, decorators, caterers, and event managers. It helps event teams turn messy client communication into organized, trackable, and professional workflows.
   The brand should sit between two worlds:
   Luxury event planning Elegant, polished, warm, refined, client-facing, premium.
   Operational SaaS clarity Structured, fast, reliable, dashboard-driven, organized, professional.
   EventSense should not feel like a generic AI chatbot. It should feel like a calm, intelligent operations command center for high-end event teams.
   Target Audience
   Primary users:
   Wedding planners
   Event agencies
   Venue coordinators
   Decorators
   Caterers
   Event managers
   Operations managers
   Client service teams
   Secondary users:
   Agency owners
   Team leads
   Sales coordinators
   Hospitality service teams
   Brand Values
   ValueMeaningClarityHelps teams understand what matters immediately.TrustMakes client communication safer and more reliable.EleganceFeels premium enough for wedding and event businesses.Calm ControlReduces stress during high-pressure planning.OrganizationTurns scattered messages into structured work.Professional ServiceSupports polished, client-ready communication.WarmthFeels human and service-oriented, not cold or robotic.
   Visual Personality
   EventSense should feel:
   Premium but not flashy
   Elegant but not decorative
   Calm but not boring
   Modern but not futuristic
   Professional but not corporate-heavy
   Warm but not childish
   Organized but not rigid
   Design Keywords
   Elegant, calm, premium, refined, structured, warm, trustworthy, polished, organized, event-focused, hospitality-inspired.
   Emotional Tone
   Users should feel:
   “Everything is under control.”
   “Important client issues will not be missed.”
   “My team looks more professional.”
   “This feels built for event businesses.”
   “This dashboard is calm even when the event is chaotic.”
2. Design Principles
3. Calm Over Clutter
   Every screen should reduce mental load. Use spacing, hierarchy, grouping, and clear labels to make complex operations feel manageable.
4. Premium Utility
   The product must look refined, but every visual choice should support usability. Decoration should never compete with task completion.
5. Soft Structure
   Use rounded cards, subtle borders, light shadows, and organized grids. The interface should feel structured without becoming cold.
6. Risk Should Be Clear, Not Loud
   Urgent issues must be visible, but alerts should feel professional. Avoid aggressive red-heavy screens unless the case is truly critical.
7. Human Communication First
   Since the product manages client messages, the interface should make conversations feel readable, respectful, and easy to act on.
8. Event-Inspired, Not Wedding-Cliché
   Use subtle inspiration from invitations, timelines, calendars, cards, arches, and soft sparkle details. Avoid overusing flowers, hearts, rings, or overly romantic visuals.

5. UI Components
   Sidebar Navigation, Cards (Radius 16px, Shadow 0 8px 24px rgba(23, 32, 51, 0.06)), Tables, Chat/Message Panels, Task Cards, Status Badges, Alerts, Buttons (Radius 12px), Inputs (Radius 12px), Modals (Radius 20px).
6. Iconography
   Lucide Icons. Outline style, 1.75px or 2px stroke.
7. Visual Motifs
   Calendar Grid Motif, Timeline Lines, Elegant Sparkle Detail, Soft Arches, Invitation-Inspired Frames, Conversation Bubble Geometry, Checklist Pattern.
8. Logo Direction
   Refined event card/calendar mark merged with a subtle conversation shape and a small champagne sparkle accent.
9. Light and Dark Mode Guidance
   Light Mode: Warm ivory backgrounds, white cards, navy text.
   Dark Mode: Deep navy surfaces, soft ivory text, muted champagne.
10. Spacing and Layout
    8px spacing system. Radius scale: 8px (sm), 12px (md), 16px (lg), 20px (xl).
